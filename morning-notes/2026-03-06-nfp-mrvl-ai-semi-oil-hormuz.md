## Top Call

**All Eyes on NFP at 8:30 — A Soft Print Could Be the Catalyst That Breaks the Oil-Driven Stalemate**

The week closes with the highest-impact data release of the month: February nonfarm payrolls at 8:30 AM ET. Consensus is just +60,000 — well below January's 130,000 and distorted by a ~31,000 hit from the California nurses' strike. A soft print would paradoxically be bullish for equities: it gives the Fed the cover to cut rates, offsetting the oil inflation narrative. A hot print above 150,000 is the bear scenario — it removes the Fed put just as Brent breaks $85. Meanwhile, last night's earnings were genuinely strong: Marvell surged +11.6% after hours on an AI semi beat, joining Broadcom's $22B Q2 guide in confirming the AI infrastructure supercycle is intact. The geopolitical backdrop remains the dominant macro risk — Iran hit an oil tanker Thursday, pushing WTI to $81 — but within that, AI infrastructure is the only sector printing positive earnings surprises consistently.

## Volatility Watch

**VIX Back to 22.5 — NFP Could Push It Either Way by 4 Points**

The VIX is creeping back toward 23 after dipping toward 21 mid-week. Today's NFP creates a binary: a soft print (+60K or below) would confirm Fed-cut optionality, likely pulling VIX back to the 19–20 zone and sparking a risk-on move in tech and semis. A hot print (+150K+) removes the Fed put in an oil-inflationary environment — VIX likely spikes to 25–27 range. Either way, implied volatility is expensive ahead of the release — do not initiate naked long vol positions. If you own positions, today is a day to size conservatively through 8:30 AM.

### Earnings Spotlight — Marvell (MRVL): The Third AI Semi Domino Falls

MRVL Q4 FY2026 revenue of $2.22B and Q1 FY2027 guide of $2.4B (vs $2.28B expected) complete the AI semi hat-trick this week: NVDA guides $78B, AVGO guides $22B, MRVL guides $2.4B. Three consecutive beats and upside guides from the three companies that build the computing, networking, and custom silicon for AI hyperscaler clusters. This is not coincidence — it is a synchronized signal from the demand side that hyperscaler capex ($200B+ combined for 2026) is flowing through to the supply chain. MRVL's custom AI accelerator (XPU) business is scaling faster than its own internal targets. The stock's +11.6% after-hours move will lift AMD, CRDO, and the broader SOX at the open.
